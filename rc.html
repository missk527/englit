Practice Questions (3)

1) How does Dickens use foreshadowing early in the novella to prepare us for Scrooge’s redemption?
Consider the images “solitary as an oyster” and Fan’s line that their father is “much kinder than he used to be,” and explain how each hints at change.
2) Compare Dickens’ presentation of weather in Stave One and Stave Five. What is the effect on the theme?
Explain how pathetic fallacy mirrors Scrooge’s inner state and reinforces the completeness of his transformation.
3) Track the shift in narrative voice from the opening to the ending. Why does this matter for the theme of change?
Discuss the unsympathetic opening description versus the jubilant tone and “splendid...most illustrious laugh” in Stave Five.
