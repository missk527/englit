Practice Question
How does Browning use structure and form to reveal the Duke’s character in My Last Duchess? Refer to iambic pentameter, the single stanza, enjambment, caesura, and rhyming couplets.
0 words
Suggested points to include:
  • Iambic pentameter echoes aristocratic control and tradition; a measured, “composed” exterior.
  • Single stanza / no breaks mirrors the Duke’s dominance and unbroken control of the conversation.
  • Enjambment creates a flowing, pressuring voice; shows his lack of self-control beneath polish.
  • Caesura (commas, dashes, parentheses) show interruptions and self-qualifications — an anxious, obsessive speaker.
  • Rhyming (heroic) couplets mostly hidden by enjambment — suggests tight control masked as “natural speech.”
  • Overall: form presents a courteous surface with coercive power underneath.
