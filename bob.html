Key Points Summary Notes

  1. Bob Cratchit represents the Victorian lower class
    • Introduced as “the clerk,” showing loss of individuality.
    • Shows struggles of working-class families.
  2. Dickens challenges stereotypes about the poor
    • Bob hardworking, grateful, compassionate.
    • Family loving and united despite poverty.
  3. Bob’s home reveals his true character
    • Loving father and husband.
    • Warm home contrasts Scrooge’s loneliness.
  4. Poverty shown through Bob
    • Low wages, mistreatment, hardship.
    • Family disproves prejudice that poor are lazy.
  5. Bob embodies religious and moral goodness
    • Moved by Tiny Tim’s Christian message.
    • Moral integrity highlighted.
  6. Family is central to Bob
    • Shows unity, appreciation, warmth.
    • Values and praises his children.
  7. Bob’s kindness highlights Scrooge’s cruelty
    • Made to work in “a dismal little cell.”
    • Still toasts Scrooge as “Founder of the Feast.”
  8. Bob’s relationship with Tiny Tim builds sympathy
    • Tiny Tim symbolises impact of poverty.
    • Bob’s grief shows emotional strength.
  9. Bob undergoes subtle development
    • Life improves after Scrooge changes.
    • Bob stays consistently good.
  10. Central to Dickens’ moral message
    • Represents goodness, generosity, humility.
    • Encourages compassion and social responsibility.
1
Who does Bob Cratchit represent?
Answer
Victorian lower class; poor workers.
2
What stereotype does Dickens challenge?
Answer
Poor people are hardworking and grateful.
3
What does Bob’s home reveal?
Answer
He is a loving father; contrasts Scrooge.
4
How is poverty shown through Bob?
Answer
Low wages, mistreatment—but hardworking family.
5
How does Bob show moral goodness?
Answer
He is religious and morally upright.
6
Why is family central to Bob?
Answer
Unity, love, appreciation for family.
7
How does Bob’s kindness highlight Scrooge’s cruelty?
Answer
Scrooge mistreats him, but Bob forgives him.
8
What does Bob’s relationship with Tiny Tim show?
Answer
Builds sympathy; Tiny Tim shows effects of poverty.
9
How does Bob develop across the novella?
Answer
Life improves, but Bob stays good.
10
Why is Bob central to Dickens’ message?
Answer
Represents goodness, compassion, moral responsibility.
