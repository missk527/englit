Key Point

What central theme underpins the novella?

Frame the whole text in one line.

Answer

Poverty & Social Injustice

The theme drives the plot, characters, and message.

Key Point

Why does Dickens spotlight poverty?

His aim for readers & society.

Answer

To drive ethical, social change

He believed literature should provoke responsibility.

Key Point

How is poverty introduced in Stave One?

Look for first clear signal.

Answer

Charity Collectors

They expose the “poor and destitute,” setting the tone.

Key Point

What does Scrooge’s early attitude represent?

Think class views in Victorian society.

Answer

Upper-class selfishness

His Malthusian view labels the poor “surplus population.”

Key Point

What’s the significance of Scrooge’s change?

Consider the message after redemption.

Answer

Social Responsibility as Ideal

Post-redemption, he models generosity and care.

Key Point

What does Tiny Tim’s fate demonstrate?

Think practical effects of action.

Answer

Charity Saves Lives

Without Scrooge’s help, Tim would have died.

Key Point

How does the setting reinforce the theme?

Notice the atmosphere and imagery.

Answer

Gloom Mirrors Society

“Fog,” “darkness,” “filth” expose poverty’s realities.

Key Point

How are stereotypes about the poor challenged?

Look for tone toward lower classes.

Answer

Humanity & Cheerfulness

Despite hardship, people “laughed heartily.”

Key Point

What do Ignorance & Want / Old Joe show?

Think symbolism & consequences.

Answer

Neglect & Consequence

Society’s abandonment + poverty’s link to crime.

Key Point

What do the Cratchits reveal?

Consider fear, scarcity, and class dynamics.

Answer

Life on the Edge

They show insecurity; Scrooge’s raise models responsibility.

Key Point

What’s the core message?

Sum it all up.

Answer

Collective Duty

Society must care for its most vulnerable.
