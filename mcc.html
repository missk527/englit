Practice exam-style questions

Use these questions to plan or write longer answers using the flashcards as support.

  1. How does Dickens use minor characters in A Christmas Carol to highlight Scrooge’s flaws and encourage his transformation? In your answer, you should write about at least three different minor characters.
  2. “In A Christmas Carol, the minor characters are the key to Dickens’ message about poverty and social responsibility.” To what extent do you agree? Refer to a range of minor characters and explore how they present Victorian society.
  3. Explore how Dickens uses the Cratchit family and one other group of minor characters to challenge Victorian stereotypes about the poor. You may wish to write about language, structure and the impact on a nineteenth-century reader.
