1. Fezziwig’s Essence
What does Fezziwig represent?

Fezziwig embodies warmth, joy, and the true spirit of Christmas, standing as a vivid contrast to Scrooge’s coldness.

2. Influence on Scrooge
How does he affect Scrooge?

His kindness towards young Scrooge sparks Scrooge’s emotional awakening and reminds him that he once valued generosity over wealth.

3. Moral Capitalism
What does he symbolise in context?

Fezziwig symbolises a humane, pre-industrial workplace and Dickens’ idea of moral capitalism—using wealth to promote happiness and community.

4. Christmas Party
Why is the party important?

Fezziwig’s Christmas Eve party shows community, generosity, and social harmony, uniting people of different classes in shared joy.

5. Ethical Leadership
How does he use power?

Fezziwig demonstrates ethical leadership: he has the power to make work “a pleasure or a toil” and chooses kindness and responsibility over exploitation.

6. Foil to Scrooge
How does he contrast with Scrooge?

As an employer who values people over profit, Fezziwig acts as a foil to Scrooge, highlighting Scrooge’s coldness and later change.

7. Relationships
How does he treat others?

Fezziwig is a fatherly figure who, along with Mrs Fezziwig, treats apprentices like family and recognises them individually.

8. Dickens’ Message
What bigger idea does he show?

Fezziwig represents Dickens’ message that true wealth lies in generosity and human connection, not in money alone.

Key Notes on Mr Fezziwig

Fezziwig’s Warm and Joyful Character

  • Described with a “jovial” and cheerful voice.
  • Represents the true spirit of Christmas through generosity and happiness.
  • Strong contrast to Scrooge’s later coldness.

Fezziwig’s Impact on Young Scrooge

  • His kindness sparks Scrooge’s emotional awakening.
  • Scrooge recognises that “the happiness he gives is quite as great as if it cost a fortune.”
  • Shows Scrooge once valued kindness before greed.

Historical and Social Context

  • Symbolises a pre-industrial, humane workplace.
  • Contrasts with harsh factory conditions of the Industrial Revolution.
  • Represents Dickens’ belief in moral capitalism.

The Christmas Party Scene

  • Fezziwig joyfully shuts the shop to celebrate Christmas.
  • Creates community among apprentices, servants, and tradespeople.
  • Symbolises Dickens’ ideal society: inclusive and joyful.

Fezziwig as a Moral Leader

  • Scrooge notes he “had the power to render us happy or unhappy.”
  • Shows leadership based on respect, kindness, and humanity.
  • Highlights the ethical responsibility of employers.

Contrast Between Fezziwig and Scrooge

  • Fezziwig values people over profit; Scrooge values money over people.
  • Fezziwig’s warmth exposes Scrooge’s selfishness.
  • Shows what Scrooge must regain to be redeemed.

Fezziwig’s Relationships

  • A fatherly figure to young Scrooge.
  • Mrs Fezziwig shares the same generosity.
  • Treats apprentices like family and knows them “by name.”

Overall Importance

  • A symbol of generosity, community, and moral goodness.
  • Provides the emotional foundation for Scrooge’s transformation.
  • Represents Dickens’ model of humane leadership.

Practice Questions

1. How does Dickens use the character of Mr Fezziwig to criticise the values of the Industrial Revolution and promote his own moral beliefs about work, generosity, and community?

2. In what ways does Fezziwig act as a foil to Scrooge, and how does this contrast help shape the reader’s understanding of Scrooge’s journey towards redemption?

3. Consider the presentation of Fezziwig’s Christmas party. How does Dickens use this scene to create an idealised vision of social harmony, and why is this moment so important for Scrooge’s emotional transformation?
