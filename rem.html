“Remember” — Christina Rossetti

Key Point Flashcards

Brief Summary

  • Speaker bids farewell to a beloved.
  • Asks to be remembered after death.
  • Prioritises the beloved’s happiness.
Flip for details

Essential idea

  • A parting note: sorrowful but tender.
  • Remembrance matters—yet not at the cost of grief.
  • Classic Victorian sonnet about love, memory, death.

Context

  • Rossetti (1830–1894), London; Pre-Raphaelite circle.
  • Wrote poem at 19 (1842); later published in Goblin Market and Other Poems.
  • Victorian mourning culture; religious undertones.
Flip for more

Why it matters

  • Italian influences (Petrarch, Dante).
  • Illness & family hardship heighten focus on death and memory.

Form & Structure

  • Petrarchan sonnet: octave + sestet.
  • Volta at line 9 (“Yet…”): request softens.
  • Rhyme: ABBA ABBA CDD ECE.
Flip for effect

So what?

  • Octave: urgent plea to “remember”.
  • Sestet: selfless shift—beloved may “forget and smile”.
  • Form mirrors emotional evolution.

Meter & Sound

  • Iambic pentameter throughout.
  • Soft alliteration (h/f) adds tenderness.
  • Strict rhythm = controlled emotion.
Flip for examples

Notable moments

  • Anadiplosis: “gone away / gone far away”.
  • Euphemism: “silent land” for death/afterlife.

Theme: Love

  • Deep, compassionate, selfless.
  • Prefers beloved’s joy over eternal remembrance.
  • Love as subtle, lasting influence.
Flip for evidence

Evidence & effect

  • “Forget and smile” > “remember and be sad”.
  • Reframing love: presence via influence, not grief.

Theme: Memory & Death

  • Death = distance (“silent land”).
  • Memory as bridge between worlds.
  • Fear of being forgotten yields to acceptance.
Flip for key lines

Key lines

  • “Only remember me; you understand / It will be late to counsel then or pray.”
  • Later: acceptance of forgetting to avoid grief.

Language & Devices

  • Direct address; intimate pronouns (you/I).
  • Repetition of “remember (me)”.
  • Caesura emphasises distance; enjambment builds flow.
Flip for interpretations

Interpretations

  • Shifting placement of “remember” mirrors waning insistence.
  • Palindromic ABBA patterns echo hesitation/return.

Comparisons

  • La Belle Dame Sans Merci: memory & death; obsession vs balance.
  • To His Coy Mistress: urgency; mortality imagery.
  • Sonnet 116: enduring, transforming love.
Flip for how to use

Use in essays

  • Contrast treatments of death/grief.
  • Compare sonnet structures and turns.
  • Track how love resists or yields to time.

Critical Perspectives

  • Feminist lens: patriarchal dynamics implied.
  • Religious lens: afterlife & consolation.
  • Formalist lens: meaning through sonnet craft.

Prompts

Practice question

Essay prompt: How does Rossetti use the Petrarchan sonnet form in Remember to chart the speaker’s shift from insistence on remembrance to selfless acceptance? In your response, analyse structure (octave/sestet, volta), sound (meter/alliteration), and key language (e.g., “silent land”, repetition of “remember”), and compare briefly with one other poem that treats love and mortality.

  2. Include a short comparative paragraph (e.g., with Sonnet 116 or To His Coy Mistress).
  3. Conclude by evaluating how form amplifies meaning.
