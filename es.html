1. Who is Ebenezer Scrooge?
Key facts about Scrooge's role in the novella.
1. Who is Ebenezer Scrooge?
  • Protagonist of A Christmas Carol.
  • Wealthy banker / moneylender with a counting house.
  • Former business partner of Jacob Marley.
2. Scrooge at the Start
How is Scrooge first presented?
2. Scrooge at the Start
  • Miserly, misanthropic and cold-hearted.
  • Hates Christmas and rejects charity.
  • Described with harsh verbs: “squeezing, wrenching, grasping, scraping, clutching, covetous old sinner”.
3. Overall Character Arc
What happens to Scrooge across the novella?
3. Overall Character Arc
  • Moves from selfish and isolated to kind and generous.
  • Goes on a journey of redemption guided by three Spirits.
  • Ends as a man who “knew how to keep Christmas well”.
4. Stave 1 – “Cold” Scrooge
How is Scrooge shown in Stave One?
4. Stave 1 – “Cold” Scrooge
  • Linked to the weather: “the cold within him”.
  • Archetypal villain; rejects charity and family.
  • Marley’s Ghost warns him to change.
5. Stave 2 – Looking Back
What does Scrooge learn from the Ghost of Christmas Past?
5. Stave 2 – Looking Back
  • Sees lonely childhood and kinder younger self.
  • Regrets rejecting the boy singing carols and mistreating Bob.
  • Realises his greed cost him Belle and happiness.
6. Stave 3 – Growing Empathy
How does the Ghost of Christmas Present change Scrooge?
6. Stave 3 – Growing Empathy
  • Sees the Cratchit family’s warmth despite poverty.
  • Develops concern for Tiny Tim’s future.
  • Begins to feel new compassion and guilt.
7. Stave 4 – Consequences
What does the Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come show him?
7. Stave 4 – Consequences
  • His lonely death and people stealing his possessions.
  • No one mourns him; businessmen are indifferent.
  • Sees Tiny Tim’s possible death; begs to change his fate.
8. Stave 5 – Transformation
How is Scrooge different at the end?
8. Stave 5 – Transformation
  • Joyful and generous: “I’m quite a baby”.
  • Donates to charity and helps the Cratchits.
  • Becomes “a second father” to Tiny Tim.
9. Scrooge and Poverty / Charity
How do Scrooge’s views on the poor change?
9. Scrooge and Poverty / Charity
  • At first, suggests prisons and workhouses for the poor.
  • Later feels shame when his words are repeated by the Spirits.
  • Ends up giving generously and supporting those in need.
10. Family and Isolation
What do we learn about Scrooge’s relationships?
10. Family and Isolation
  • “Solitary as an oyster” – chooses isolation.
  • Rejects Fred’s invitations and ignores his family.
  • Later reconnects with family and community, finding happiness.
11. Materialism
How is Scrooge’s obsession with money shown?
11. Materialism
  • Described as a “man of worldly mind”.
  • Belle leaves him for worshipping a “golden idol”.
  • His wealth is “of no use to him” if he does no good with it.
12. Key Quotations
Essential quotes to remember for Scrooge.
12. Key Quotations
  • “A squeezing, wrenching, grasping, scraping, clutching, covetous old sinner!”
  • “Solitary as an oyster.”
  • “I’m quite a baby.”

Notes – Key Points for Your Scrooge Speech

1. Who Scrooge Is

  • Main character of A Christmas Carol.
  • A miserly, cold-hearted businessman at the start.
  • Described using “cold” metaphors.

2. His Initial Attitude

  • Hates Christmas and avoids people.
  • No compassion for the poor (“prisons”).
  • Isolated — even animals avoid him.

3. Purpose of His Character

  • Shows anyone can change.
  • Represents compassion and social responsibility.

4. Transformation

  • Past: Regret.
  • Present: Empathy.
  • Future: Consequences.
  • Final: Generous.

5. Themes

  • Redemption
  • Poverty & responsibility
  • Family
  • Materialism
  • Christmas spirit
  • Isolation

6. Relationships

  • Bob Cratchit
  • Fred
  • Fezziwig
  • Tiny Tim

7. Key Quotes

  • “A squeezing, wrenching…”
  • “Solitary as an oyster.”
  • “I’m quite a baby.”

8. Final Message

  • Model of Christmas spirit & community.
