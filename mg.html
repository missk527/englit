Study Notes

1. Marley’s Ghost as a Character

  • First ghost to visit Scrooge.
  • Reflects Scrooge’s own character and possible future.
  • Former business partner and close companion.

2. Symbolism and Moral Meaning

  • Represents Christian ideas of purgatory, punishment, and redemption.
  • His chains symbolize sins and greed: “cash boxes, keys, padlocks, ledgers, deeds.”
  • Shows the consequences of selfishness.

3. Purpose in the Story

  • Introduces supernatural and religious themes.
  • Announces the arrival of the Three Spirits (story structure).
  • Acts as a warning of Scrooge’s potential fate.
  • Begins Scrooge’s transformation.

4. Relationship with Scrooge

  • Scrooge was Marley’s “sole friend” and “sole mourner.”
  • Shared values make Marley’s warning powerful.
  • Shows emotional emptiness in their lives.

5. Main Messages Marley Delivers

  • People are responsible for their actions.
  • Social responsibility > wealth: “Mankind was my business.”
  • Redemption is possible through change.

6. Effect on Scrooge

  • Instills fear and forces self-reflection.
  • Opens him to lessons from the three spirits.

Who is Marley to Scrooge?

His former business partner and closest companion.

Why does Marley appear?

To warn Scrooge about the consequences of his behaviour.

What do Marley’s chains symbolise?

The sins, greed, and choices he made in life.

What theme does Marley introduce?

The supernatural and moral accountability.

What does Marley warn Scrooge will happen?

That Scrooge will share his fate if he doesn’t change.

What structural role does Marley play?

He announces the three spirits, setting up the story.

What is Marley’s message about wealth?

That caring for mankind is more important than money.

How does Scrooge react after Marley leaves?

He feels terrified and shaken, beginning to doubt himself.

Why is Marley a mirror to Scrooge?

Their shared attitudes mean Scrooge could repeat Marley’s fate.

What emotion does Marley use to change Scrooge?

Fear—forcing Scrooge to reflect and begin changing.

  2. In what ways does Marley act as both a warning and a catalyst for Scrooge’s transformation?
  3. Discuss how Marley’s relationship with Scrooge helps highlight the novella’s message about human connection and compassion.
